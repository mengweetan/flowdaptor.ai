The premise

Most "AI products" are rentable demos. We build operating systems.

The market is flooded with SaaS automation tools — slick interfaces, monthly fees, and a quiet expectation that your business adapts to their model. We took the opposite view: real businesses need automation that adapts to their model, runs on their servers, and stays under their control. Open-source n8n made that possible. We made it deployable.

Operating principles

Five things we don't compromise on.

Client owns everything

Source, infrastructure, runbooks. We design for handover from the first hour.

Production over demo

Anything we ship has retries, error handling, observability, and a runbook. If it can't survive a Tuesday, we don't deploy it.

Scoped honestly

If a workflow is the wrong shape for automation, we say so. Bad scoping creates bad systems; honesty is cheaper than rework.

Teach as we build

Every engagement leaves the client team more capable than we found them. Documentation and training are baked in, not bolted on.
